Variable input size (food items)

1. Fixed-size state representation:
Instead of representing each food item individually, you could create a fixed-size representation of the environment.
For example, you could divide the space into a grid and represent the reward density in each grid cell.
Or you could represent the N closest food items, where N is a fixed number.
Attention mechanisms:
These allow the network to focus on relevant parts of a variable-sized input.
You could represent all food items and use an attention mechanism to weight their importance.
Graph Neural Networks:
These can handle varying numbers of nodes (food items) and their relationships.
Each food item could be a node, with edges representing distances between items.
Recurrent Neural Networks:
These can process sequences of varying length.
You could "scan" the environment, processing food items one at a time and updating a hidden state.
Convolutional Neural Networks:
If you represent the environment as a 2D grid, CNNs can extract relevant features regardless of the number of food items.
